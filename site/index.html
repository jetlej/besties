You probably have questions.

Why does it need Full Disk Access?

Your Mac stores your iMessage history in a local database that only apps with Full Disk Access may read — that's the only door Apple provides. Besties opens that database read-only, on your machine, and nothing else. You can verify every byte of that claim in the source code.

Does anything get uploaded, ever?

No. The app contains no networking code at all — no analytics, no telemetry, no "anonymous usage data." It works identically with your internet turned off.

Which messaging apps does it support?

iMessage and WhatsApp. Besties reads the history both apps already keep on your Mac and merges each person's threads into one timeline — so a friend you text on both shows up as one person, one history.

Can it change or delete my messages?

No. Besties opens the Messages database in read-only mode — it's technically incapable of writing to it. Your conversations can't be modified, reordered, or deleted by the app.

How do I install it?

Open the download and drag Besties into the Applications folder — the classic Mac install. Open it and it walks you through granting Full Disk Access (about 20 seconds in System Settings), then starts reading your history immediately.

My history doesn't go back very far — why?

Besties can only see the messages your Mac has. If you have "Messages in iCloud" turned on, your Mac usually holds your full history. If your history looks short, enable Messages in iCloud on your Mac and give it time to sync, then relaunch the app.

What about group chats?

Besties focuses on your one-on-one conversations — the relationships. Group chats aren't included right now.

What does it run on?

macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later, on Apple Silicon. It reads the Messages history already synced to your Mac.

Is it really yours forever?

Yes. Pay once, use it forever — no subscription, no account, no license server that could ever shut off. The app is a file on your Mac, and it stays yours.
